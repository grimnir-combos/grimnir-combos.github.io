Grimnir Combos

Midscreen Setups

Meterless Midscreen

(starter) > 214L

Grimnir's meterless midscreen conversions are pretty average. Off very distant hits (including most whiff punishes) you can't even connect 214L. If your starter is autocombo, you can end on 214M instead for greater corner carry.

Simple Midscreen

autocombo or 2M > 236H~8 > 214U > 2H > 623M > 2M > 214H

Your easiest (actual) midscreen combo. If you're dropping the 2M after 623M it's probably because you're doing either the 214U or the 2H too late. You want all inputs as early as possible.

If you reach the corner for wallbounce you gain a lot of flexibility with the ender. The one shown in the video is 5L > 22H, which leaves you +1 in their face. Note that any button you press here will lose to wakeup throw. Set up single seal instead if you want to safely take pressure with a button.

If you're close enough to the corner after the 2M you can press 623H > j.22H instead to safely set up seals.

If you're a little spaced out of the corner, you get a higher wallbounce that leaves you enough time to end in 22U for a safejump setup.

Midscreen Safejump (f.L)

autocombo or 2M > 236H~8 > 214U > 22U > f.L > 623L (> 9U whiff > j.H safejump)

Your easiest midscreen safejump setup. Has less corner carry than the previous combo and spends an additional 25%, but gives you a safejump from anywhere on screen. Also leaves a little bit of room for wacky shenanigans, which are covered in more detail on other pages.

Midscreen Safejump (929U)

autocombo or 2M > 236H~8 > 214U > (delay)22U > c.H > 623L > 9U > 2U (> delay 9U whiff > j.H safejump)

Delay the 9U whiff at the end very slightly for this easy safejump setup. Its big appeal over the previous setup is the high ambiguity in the timing, which opens the possibility for a tricky empty jump low.

Midscreen Safejump (92U6U)

autocombo or 2M > 236H~8 > 214U > (delay)22U > 5H > 623L > 9U > (delay)j.2U (> rejump j.6U whiff > j.M safejump)

Delay the j.2U a little bit or it won't be a safejump. In fact, if you do the j.2U instantly you'll even lose to wakeup jab. Has the most corner carry of the midscreen safejump routes, though only by a little bit.

Midscreen Crossup (22[L])

autocombo or 2M > 236H~8 > 214U > (delay)22U > c.H > 623L > 9U > j.M (> 22[L] > j.6U whiff > j.M)

Can crossup depending on your timings, which is funny but often results in you getting brave countered straight into the corner. To avoid crossing up delay the j.M and 22[L] cancel slightly. Crossup is a safejump, sameside is not.

Midscreen 623L

623L (reaches corner)

(starter) > 623L > 6U > 2M > 236H > run forward > 214H > 623H > j.H > rejump j.H (> j.22H > j.6U whiff > j.M safejump)

When you hit your starter but don't quite reach your seal for a gale dash extension, you can use the forward movement from light spin to convert into a full combo. If you think you'll reach the corner with 214H, this sets up for a meterless safejump. Need to eyeball the delay on the 623H based on distance from corner; sending it as early as possible won't let you connect j.H after. If you aren't comfortable with guessing the timing for the safejump, you can just do 5H > 623H > j.22H instead to set up seals. This is preferable to dropping the combo.

623L (no corner)

(starter) > 623L > 6U > 2M > 236H > run forward > 214U > 2H > 623H > 22X

If you don't think you'll reach the corner with 214H, you can do this instead to push your opponent Quite Close to the corner and set up seals. Running forward is optional but gives additional corner carry, and honestly it helps with the timing.

623M (reaches corner)

(starter) > 623M > j.3U > 5H > 214H > 623H > j.H > rejump j.H (> j.22H > j.6U whiff > j.M safejump)

A similar concept with 623M. Less damage than the 623L route but keeps your tornado cooldown. Generally you don't get the choice of which route you use because it depends where your seals are, but on the rare occasions you do get to choose, that's your tradeoff.

623M (reaches corner)

(starter) > 623M > j.3U > 5H > 236H~8 > 214U > 2H > 623H > 22X

Similar variant if you won't reach the corner with 214H.

Bonus Routes

These setups are either more difficult or less useful than their alternatives.

Midscreen to Corner Safejump

autocombo or 2M > 236H~8 > 214U > 22U > 5H > 623L > 9U > j.M (> j.9U6U whiff > j.H safejump)

If 214U takes you close-ish to the corner, you can spend three of your 22U seals to go into this easy safejump setup. Orrrrrr, you could spend one of your 22U seals and do the f.L > 9U whiff safejump setup instead. This was much more useful on an old patch where fuzzies were still in the game. These days, you won't find much practical use for it beyond "I want to do a different combo."

Midscreen Safejump (76U)

autocombo or 2M > 236H~8 > 214U > (delay)22U > c.H > 623L > 9U > (delay)j.M (> j.7U6U whiff > j.H fuzzy)

The rare 7 input. Fairly difficult setup due to the awkward j.M link. Not really any concrete reason to go for this over one of the easier setups, but it looks pretty cool.
